Explore the Constitution!

The Constitution is like the ultimate rulebook for our country. It tells us how our government works and protects our rights. Let’s break it down into easy, fun parts!

The Preamble

The Preamble is the opening statement of the Constitution. It tells us why the Constitution was written and what it's meant to achieve. It’s like a mission statement!

The Bill of Rights

The Bill of Rights includes the first 10 amendments of the Constitution. These amendments protect your freedom, including the right to speak your mind, practice your own religion, and more!

How Laws Are Made

Ever wonder how laws come to be? The Constitution sets out how laws are made and approved by Congress, making sure every new law is fair for everyone.

Fun Facts

  • The Constitution was written over 230 years ago, but it still affects us today!
  • There are 27 amendments to the Constitution. The first 10 are the Bill of Rights.
  • The longest-serving Chief Justice was John Marshall, who served for 34 years!
  • The original Constitution was signed on September 17, 1787, and is still on display in Washington, D.C.
  • James Madison, known as the "Father of the Constitution," played a key role in drafting and promoting it.
